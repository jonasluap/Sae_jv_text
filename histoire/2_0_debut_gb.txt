It is 2050, and the new government has hired private researchers across the country to conduct experiments on mice in order to find a cure for diabetes.
If a cure is found, it will give the state a good image and ensure its longevity, given the current context, which is conducive to revolution. There is a lot of poverty and unemployment.
You are one of these researchers, working in Calais, in a hospital that is a little way out of town but still takes in some patients.
Your manager has ordered you to start the experiments, but you know that the stocks of medicine provided by the state are limited due to their low budget.
All use of materials must be reported and recorded in a digital file that precisely lists each vial used.
Among the substances at your disposal is a recently synthesized experimental compound:
SIS-BETA.
Its origin is unclear. Its potential is immense.
But its side effects are unknown.

What do you decide to do?

	1: Immediately inject a full dose of SIS-BETA into the mouse, without conducting preliminary tests, in order to save time.

	2: Dilute the SIS-BETA substance and perform a partial injection, while carefully recording the operation in the digital file.
